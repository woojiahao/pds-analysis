After analyzing this data set. I managed to confirm my original hypothesis, which was that the declining rate of primary enrolment is caused by the declining population.

However, there was 1 peculiar observation in the dataset, notice how from 1986 to 1988, there was a sudden spike in the number of live births, from 38,379 to 52,957.

This sudden spike is attributed to the establishment of the Inter-Ministerial Population Committee in 1986, and later on, the introduction of the Three or more if you can afford it policy that was implemented in 1987. This actually aligns well with the changes in the live births rate.

Year Number of live births
1986 38,379
1987 43,616
1988 52,957

Table: Spikes in live birth rates between 1986 and 1988


Other spikes could also be attributed to other reasons, such as the spike from 1999 to 2000 caused by the year of 2000 being the Year of the Dragon which is an auspicious year in which more Chinese couples will try for a baby.

From this data set, it is very obvious that whilst the government has been putting in place many different birth policies to encourage its citizens to give birth, most do not see a lot of success, apart from the Three or more if you can afford it policy.

Year Number of live births
1999 43,336
2000 46,997

Table: Spikes in live birth rates due to the Year of the Dragon
