In order to confirm that the cost of living is indeed increasing in Singapore, we will be analyzing the following data sets:

  1. Median resale flat price
  2. Job vacancy rate

Resale flat prices

The resale flat prices are observed has been increasing. This is a problem for couples as this will discourage them from having children since with more members in their family, they would need more apartment space, which might not be something that they are able to afford.
For instance, if a couple who already has 1 child wishes to have another child, that would mean they would need a 4-room flat since they wish to ensure both children have a conducive environment to grow up in, but moving from a 3-room flat to a 4-room flat would involve an estimated 51% increase in cost of their flat, which might not be something they are able to afford, in conjunction with feeding 4 mouths now.

Job vacancy rate

The job vacancy rate has been sharply decreasing over time, with it continually fluctuating since 2006. This might present a problem for couples since with a lower rate of job vacancy and potential loss of job security, having too many children could spell trouble for the couple if they had too many mouths to feed.
